About This Map

1689 Heritage Trail is an interactive historical atlas tracing the story of the Particular Baptists across England and Wales — from the emergence of the first congregations in the 1630s through the era of Spurgeon and the Metropolitan Tabernacle.

The map is centered on the 1689 Second London Baptist Confession of Faith, the defining confessional document of the Particular (Reformed) Baptist tradition. Drafted secretly in 1677 by Nehemiah Coxe and William Collins during fierce persecution, it was publicly adopted by over 100 churches after the Toleration Act of 1689.

Four Eras

  • ■ Origins (1612–1644) — First churches and the 1644 Confession
  • ■ Persecution (1644–1689) — The Clarendon Code and secret confession-making
  • ■ Toleration (1689–1792) — Public confession and institutional growth
  • ■ Mission (1792–1892) — Global mission and the Prince of Preachers
